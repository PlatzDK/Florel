Intro

Central Jutland is an angler’s paradise. From lake fishing in the Silkeborg Lakes, salmon fishing in Karup Å to put & take ponds and coastal fishing in the Limfjord – the Engesvang area offers something for both experienced anglers and families who want to try their luck in beautiful surroundings.

Local fishing spots

Fra sommerhuset er der kort afstand til en række populære fiskevande:

  • Bølling Sø (2 km): Frit fiskeri året rundt, især efter gedde og aborre. Mulighed for kano/robåd.
  • Silkeborgsøerne (15 km): Store søer med gedde, sandart, ørred og ål. Kræver fisketegn og dagkort.
  • Karup Å (30 km): Verdensberømt for sine kæmpe havørreder. Sæson fra april til oktober, kræver fiskekort.
  • Hjarbæk Fjord (55 km): Kystfiskeri efter havørred, hornfisk og fladfisk.
  • Skyggehale Put & Take (5 km): Familievenlig sø med udsatte ørreder, handicapvenlige faciliteter.
  • Tollundgaard Put & Take (10 km): En af Danmarks ældste put & take-søer.

Fishing with/without a boat

Want to get out on the water? In Silkeborg you can rent motorboats, canoes and kayaks. At Lake Bølling fishing from canoes and rowboats without engines is allowed. Many anglers also use belly boats or pontoons – a unique way to reach the fish.

Guides & tackle shops

For both beginners and experienced anglers, a local guide can make all the difference. Riverfisher.dk offers guided trips at the Gudenå River and Karup Å. The area also has large tackle shops: Special-Butikken in Silkeborg (15 km) and Seatrout Jagt & Fiskeri in Herning (25 km).

Clubs & competitions

The area has active fishing clubs such as the Silkeborg Angling Association and Herning-Ikast Angling Association. Guests can buy day permits and join competitions like Silkeborg’s spring pike competition or Predator Cup – Denmark’s largest freshwater fishing contest.

Nature experiences

At Lake Bølling you can hike the 12 km trail around the lake with bird towers, shelters and viewpoints. Along the Gudenå River you can go canoeing or walk the ‘Trækstien’ path and enjoy nature. The area offers stunning sunsets, misty mornings over the lake and unique nature experiences.

Practical information

For fishing in lakes and rivers, a state fishing license and often a local day ticket is required. Put & take ponds only require a ticket bought on-site. Coastal fishing only requires a state fishing license – otherwise you can fish freely from the shore.

Fishing activities

Denmark offers varied angling: coastal and sea fishing, river and lake fishing, and put & take ponds.

  • Coastal fishing for sea trout, garfish and mackerel (spring / late summer / autumn).
  • Lake fishing: pike, perch, zander, trout and eel.
  • River fishing in clear streams — local day permits often required.
  • Put & take ponds: family-friendly and usually open daily.
  • Boat, kayak or belly-boat angling where permitted.

Local fishing spots

  • Bølling Lake (≈2 km): free shore fishing; pike, perch; shelters and canoe/rowboat.
  • Skyggehale Put & Take (≈5 km): family-friendly; toilets and cleaning tables; stocked trout.
  • Silkeborg Lakes / Langsø (Gudenå) (≈15 km): large lakes; day tickets often required.
  • Tollundgaard Put & Take (Pårup) (≈10 km): classic put & take pond.
  • Karup River (≈30 km): river angling noted for sea trout — local permits required.
  • Hjarbæk Fjord (Limfjord) (≈55 km): fjord/coastal fishing (sea trout, garfish).
